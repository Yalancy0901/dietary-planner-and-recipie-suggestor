Types of diet:

1. Vegetarian:

A well-planned vegetarian diet is completely balanced. This eating pattern excludes all meat and its by-products. In some cultures, vegetarianism may mean the inclusion of eggs in diets. Fresh fruits, vegetables, cereals, legumes, nuts, and seeds are integral to a vegetarian diet. A well-balanced vegetarian diet can help you stay fit and lose weight or maintain optimum weight. It can fulfil all your nutritional requirements. A well-balanced plant-based diet often contains less saturated fat (SFAs), more folate, fibre and antioxidants. Also, vegetarians are naturally inclined to have a higher intake of fruit and vegetables, which can help meet the recommended daily allowances of micronutrients


2. Paleo diet:

The paleo diet emphasizes whole foods, lean protein, vegetables, fruits, nuts, and seeds, while discouraging processed foods, sugar, dairy, and grains. Some more flexible versions of the paleo diet also allow for dairy like cheese and butter, as well as tubers like potatoes and sweet potatoes.


3. Vegan diet:

Veganism is the strictest form of vegetarianism. In addition to eliminating meat, it eliminates dairy, eggs, and animal-derived products, such as gelatin, honey, albumin, whey, casein, and some forms of vitamin D3.


4. Gluten-free:

A gluten-free diet involves excluding foods that contain the protein gluten like wheat and rye products. Gluten is a family of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and spelt.Most whole, unprocessed foods fit into a gluten-free diet, allowing a wide variety of options.
